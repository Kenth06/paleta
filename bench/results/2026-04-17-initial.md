# Bench results — 2026-04-17 (initial)

Node v22, x86_64, vitest 2.1.9 pure-JS implementation. Not Workers isolate.
Workers isolate is V8-equivalent, so these numbers translate within ~10–20%.

## Workload: 128×128 noise (typical post-resize hot path input)

| Benchmark         | hz      | mean (ms) | p99 (ms) |
| ----------------- | ------- | --------- | -------- |
| buildHistogram    | 5,908   | 0.169     | 0.635    |
| quantizeWu(10)    | 1,891   | 0.529     | 2.588    |
| quantizeWu(16)    | 1,793   | 0.558     | 2.991    |

**Full post-decode palette extraction ≈ 0.7ms** on a 128×128 input. Meets the
v0.1 internal target of <5ms for the post-decode portion of the pipeline.

## Workload: 1024×1024 noise (full-decode, no resize path)

| Benchmark               | hz      | mean (ms) | p99 (ms) |
| ----------------------- | ------- | --------- | -------- |
| buildHistogram(step=1)  | 193     | 5.17      | 5.86     |
| buildHistogram(step=50) | 4,944   | 0.202     | 0.695    |

Stride sampling is **25.5× faster** than full scan on a 1MP image. Confirms the
pipeline's `chooseStride` default (`ceil(totalPixels / maxSamples)`) is
doing its job — after resize to ≤128×128 the histogram walks ~16k pixels,
not 1M.
